## 3 — Why Personalize? *(5:00 – 7:30)*

*[Header: "the psychology / Why Personalize?"]*

> Before we go deeper into the architecture, let's talk about *why* personalization works at all. Because it's not just about showing the right product. There's real psychology happening here.

*[Arrow → Three principle cards appear]*

> Three forces at play.
>
> **The Endowment Effect.** "It's already mine." When you get a tailored offer, a loyalty reward, a personalized recommendation — your brain starts to feel ownership *before you buy*. And here's the kicker: loss aversion is two to three times more powerful than the pleasure of gaining something new. Once someone feels like it's theirs, taking it away hurts.
>
> **Reciprocity.** "Give value, get value back." This is basic human wiring. When customers receive something genuinely useful — a relevant offer, a piece of content that actually helps — they reciprocate. With their time. Their money. Positive reviews. Word-of-mouth. Referrals. You give value, you get value back.
>
> **Belonging.** "They actually know me." When a brand makes you feel *seen* — not surveilled, but understood — that triggers identity-level loyalty. It's deeper than price. These customers don't comparison shop. They advocate.

*[Arrow → Cycle note]*

> And these aren't isolated effects. They compound. Every personalized interaction deepens the endowment effect, which increases reciprocity, which strengthens belonging, which generates more data, which enables better personalization. It's a flywheel.

*[Arrow → Stats row]*

> The numbers back it up. **80%** more likely to purchase with personalized experiences. **50%** reduction in acquisition costs. **5 to 15%** revenue lift. This isn't theoretical — this is McKinsey, Forrester, measured across thousands of companies.

Context is the *sum total* of an individual, as they appear, within your sphere of influence. It's *all* the data — stitched together, unified, real-time.

*[Arrow → Constellation SVG animates]*

> Look at this. Behaviors, purchases, interactions, preferences, content consumption, demographics, browsing patterns, device signals — all of it feeding into a single unified profile. *That's* context. Not one of these things in isolation. All of them together.

*[Arrow → "Context is currency" cards]*

> And here's the frame I want you to carry out of this room: **context is currency.**
>
> It has **value** — contextual marketing drives 43% higher purchase intent.
>
> It's **fungible** — the same context powers email, web, ads, SMS, push, in-app. Every channel.
>
> It's **exchangeable** — shared across marketing, sales, support, product. Everyone speaks the same customer language.
>
> And it **buys you things**. Like more customers.
>
> The companies winning right now aren't the ones with the most data. They're the ones with the most *context*.

## 13 — Future Puck *(23:00 – 24:30)*

*[Header: "what's next / Move to where the puck will be"]*

> Let me leave you with where this is all headed. Two capabilities that separate the next generation from the current one.

*[Arrow → Bandit Math]*

> **Bandit Math.** The multi-armed bandit is a gold-standard experimentation technique. The system automatically dials in the best variant based on conversions. Great. But it treats every profile the same. Sally — 25, single, healthy — gets the same winning message as John — 60, married, grandchildren, chronic conditions. That's the vanilla bandit. Missing context.
>
> The *contextual* bandit takes profile attributes into account. Sally gets what's optimal for *her*. John gets what's optimal for *him*. Same technique, dramatically different outcomes. That's the power of context meeting experimentation.

*[Arrow → Embed All The Things]*

> **Embed ALL THE THINGS.** Vector embeddings are quietly revolutionizing how we think about similarity. You take every product, every profile, every behavior, every piece of content — and you map them into a shared mathematical space. Similar things cluster together. Now you can do real-time similarity search across *any dimension*. "Show me customers like this one." "Find products related to this behavior." "What content resonates with this profile?" All computed in milliseconds.
>
> These two capabilities — contextual bandits and universal embeddings — are where the puck is going. The teams that invest now will have a compounding advantage.
